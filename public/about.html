About

Exhibition planning, drawn to scale

Sightlines is a planning tool for people who hang exhibitions. Draw a gallery to scale, place artworks on its walls at their real dimensions, check spacing and eye lines, and walk the result in a simple 3D preview. It all runs in the browser, with nothing to install.

Who it's for

Sightlines is built for curators, exhibition planners, registrars, preparators, artists, and small gallery or museum teams. Exhibition work is non-linear: sometimes the checklist comes first, sometimes the room does. Sightlines is designed so you can start from either end and move between checklist thinking and spatial thinking without losing precision.

What you can do with it

  • Plan view: draw rooms and walls to scale on a drafting-style grid.
  • Wall elevations: see each wall face-on and place works at exact heights, with snapping, centerlines, and measured spacing.
  • Artwork checklist: keep works, dimensions, and images together, and drag works from the list straight onto a wall.
  • 3D preview: check sightlines and proportions from a visitor's point of view.
  • Portable projects: export a project as a file to back it up, move it between machines, or hand it to a colleague.

Private by design

Sightlines doesn't ask you to create an account or upload your exhibition to anyone's server. Your project (layouts, artwork details, and images) is stored locally in your own browser, and sharing happens through files you export deliberately. Unreleased exhibition plans and artwork images are sensitive material; keeping them on your device is the point. The details are on the privacy page.

How it's built

Sightlines is a static web application served over HTTPS from sightlines.art on Cloudflare's edge network. There is no software to download or install, which also makes it practical on managed institutional machines. Technical notes for reviewers are on the security page and the IT access page.
